## Design Context

### Users
Mise en Place is for singles and couples managing recipe planning, shopping, and home cooking. They are usually using the product in practical, time-constrained moments: deciding what to cook, organizing the week, building a shopping list, or cooking from an existing plan. The interface should reduce friction and help them stay coordinated without feeling busy or demanding.

### Brand Personality
The product should feel simple, clean, and useful. Its emotional goals are calm and efficiency: users should feel oriented, capable, and lightly supported rather than excited, gamified, or overwhelmed. Copy and interaction design should be direct, reassuring, and low-drama.

### Aesthetic Direction
Keep the visual direction understated and practical. Favor clarity, restraint, and strong information hierarchy over decorative flourishes. Both light mode and dark mode should be treated as first-class experiences. There are no required brand colors, so color choices should support readability and task flow rather than become the identity themselves. Avoid interfaces that feel trendy, flashy, overly editorial, or obviously AI-generated.

### Design Principles
1. Design for calm throughput.
Prioritize quick comprehension, clear next actions, and low-cognitive-load layouts that help users move from planning to shopping to cooking smoothly.

2. Keep utility visually clean.
Use simple compositions, consistent spacing, and restrained surfaces so the product feels tidy and dependable instead of ornamental.

3. Let hierarchy do the work.
Emphasize structure, labels, and task states with typography, spacing, and grouping before reaching for stronger color or motion.

4. Treat both themes as complete products.
Every interface should work intentionally in light and dark mode, with equal attention to contrast, depth, and legibility.

5. Support real kitchen-life use.
Favor obvious controls, durable forms, readable lists, and forgiving interactions that work well during planning, shopping, and cooking contexts.
